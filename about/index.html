About Doug Bullis

I’m a Managing Broker in Washington State with a background in brokerage operations, broker development, real estate systems, and professional education.

For over 25 years, I’ve worked closely with brokers across Washington, including serving as an instructor with the NWMLS. That experience gave me direct insight into how brokers operate day to day — what works well, where friction shows up, and what it takes to build stronger, more consistent business systems.

What I Focus On

  • Broker operations and structure
  • Broker growth and business development
  • Transaction flow and process improvement
  • Systems that reduce friction and increase consistency
  • Operational clarity that supports long-term scalability

How I Think

I approach real estate through a systems and process lens. With a Lean Six Sigma background, I focus on simplifying workflows, removing inefficiencies, and building repeatable practices that improve broker performance over time.

The goal is not complexity. It’s clarity, consistency, and better outcomes.

Current Focus

I’m currently reconnecting with professionals across Washington State and exploring opportunities where I can contribute through brokerage operations, leadership, training, and process improvement.

Who I Work With

I work best with brokers who are already producing and ready to improve how they operate.

  • Brokers closing transactions without consistent systems behind their business
  • Experienced brokers who have hit a plateau and want more structure and direction
  • High-producing brokers needing stronger operational systems to support growth

In most cases, the issue isn’t effort — it’s the absence of structured systems that create consistency, efficiency, and long-term sustainability.
