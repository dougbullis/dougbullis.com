About Doug Bullis

I’m a Managing Broker in Washington State with a background in brokerage operations, broker development, real estate systems, and professional education.

Over the years, I’ve worked closely with brokers across Washington, including serving as an instructor with NWMLS. That experience gave me direct insight into how brokers actually operate day to day — what works well, where friction shows up, and what it takes to build stronger, more consistent business systems.

What I Focus On

  • Brokerage operations and structure
  • Broker growth and business development
  • Transaction flow and process improvement
  • Systems that reduce friction and increase consistency
  • Operational clarity that supports long-term scalability

How I Think

I approach real estate through a systems and process lens. With a Lean Six Sigma background, I focus on simplifying workflows, removing inefficiencies, and building repeatable practices that support better performance over time.

The goal is not complexity. It’s clarity, consistency, and better outcomes for brokers, brokerages, and the people they serve.

Current Focus

I’m currently reconnecting with professionals across Washington State and exploring opportunities where I can contribute at a higher level through brokerage leadership, operations, training, process improvement, and strategic support.

Who I Work With

I connect especially well with brokers and brokerage professionals who are capable, committed, and ready for stronger structure around how they operate.

  • Brokers who are producing but need better systems to grow more consistently
  • Experienced brokers whose business has leveled off and who want more efficiency and leverage
  • High-performing brokers who need stronger operational support behind their production
  • Brokerage leaders who value clarity, accountability, and scalable processes

In many cases, the issue is not talent or work ethic. It’s the absence of the right systems, support, and operational framework to perform at a higher level.
